Parmesan Orzo

An very hands-off starch you're able to make while you're busy making the rest of your meal. Parmesan orzo might take 20 minutes on the stove top, but you'll spend less than five working on the hot and creamy side.

Ingredients

  • 1 stick or 8 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 lb orzo
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. In a large cast iron skillet on medium high heat, put in butter and let melt, coating the entire bottom of the pan.
  2. Add in the uncooked orzo pasta and spread across the entire pan so as much pasta as possible is touching it. Wait until the orzo has started to brown and then stir. Repeat until the orzo is very multicolored.
  3. Pour in chicken broth and stir. Place a lid on top and let simmer until the orzo has absorbed the broth.
  4. Turn off the heat and pour in the center the parmesan cheese. Mix thoroughly until the cheese is well incorporated and the whole dish is creamy. Remove from stove top and serve immediately.
